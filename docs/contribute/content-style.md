# Content style

## Tone and voice
- Use plain language and short sentences.
- Write for practitioners who want to act quickly.

## Headings
- Use sentence case for headings.
- Keep headings concise and meaningful.

## Tables
- Use tables for checklists, comparisons, and templates.
- Include a short caption sentence above complex tables.

## Callouts
- Use callouts sparingly for key warnings or reminders.
- Keep callouts to one or two sentences.

## Links and citations
- Link to external publications rather than duplicating long text.
- Use clear link text that explains the destination.

## Accessibility
- Avoid dense paragraphs.
- Prefer lists for steps and requirements.
